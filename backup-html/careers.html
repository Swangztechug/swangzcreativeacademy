Career Paths in Music

Discover the diverse career opportunities available to graduates of our programs. From performing artists to sound engineers, explore the many paths your music education can take you.

Music Producer

Create and produce music for artists, films, and commercial projects. Work in studios or independently, shaping the sound of tomorrow's hits.

Average Salary: $50k - $150k+

  • • Studio production
  • • Artist collaboration
  • • Music arrangement

Sound Engineer

Mix and master audio recordings, work in live sound, or specialize in post-production for media. Technical expertise meets creative artistry.

Average Salary: $40k - $120k+

  • • Audio mixing & mastering
  • • Live sound engineering
  • • Post-production

Performing Artist

Pursue a career as a solo artist, band member, or session musician. Perform live and record albums, sharing your music with the world.

Average Salary: $30k - $200k+

  • • Live performances
  • • Recording artist
  • • Session musician

Music Teacher

Educate the next generation of musicians in schools, private studios, or online platforms. Share your passion and expertise.

Average Salary: $35k - $80k+

  • • Private instruction
  • • School music programs
  • • Online teaching

Music Composer

Write original music for films, TV shows, video games, and commercial advertising. Create soundscapes that enhance visual media.

Average Salary: $45k - $150k+

  • • Film & TV scoring
  • • Video game music
  • • Commercial composition

Music Director

Lead musical productions, conduct orchestras, or direct music programs for organizations. Combine leadership with musical expertise.

Average Salary: $50k - $100k+

  • • Orchestra conducting
  • • Music program direction
  • • Production leadership
